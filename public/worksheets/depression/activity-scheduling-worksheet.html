Activity Scheduling Worksheet

Activity scheduling is a powerful technique used in behavioral activation therapy to help manage depression. By planning and tracking daily activities, you can increase engagement in positive experiences, establish routine, and gradually improve your mood and energy levels.

How to Use This Worksheet

  1. Start by scheduling a few activities each day. Begin with small, manageable tasks.
  2. Include a mix of necessary tasks, pleasurable activities, and activities that give you a sense of mastery or accomplishment.
  3. For each activity, rate your anticipated enjoyment and mastery before doing it (0-10).
  4. After completing the activity, rate your actual enjoyment and mastery (0-10).
  5. Note any observations or thoughts about the activity.
  6. Gradually increase the number and complexity of activities as your energy and motivation improve.

Pleasure Activities

Activities that bring enjoyment or positive emotions

  • Listening to music
  • Taking a warm bath
  • Watching a favorite show
  • Reading for pleasure
  • Spending time in nature

Mastery Activities

Activities that give a sense of accomplishment

  • Completing household chores
  • Learning a new skill
  • Exercising
  • Finishing a work task
  • Organizing a space

Social Connection

Activities that involve interacting with others

  • Calling a friend
  • Having coffee with someone
  • Attending a group activity
  • Family meals
  • Video calls with loved ones

Tips for Successful Activity Scheduling

  • Start small: Begin with just a few activities that feel manageable.
  • Be specific: Instead of "exercise," write "15-minute walk around the block."
  • Balance your activities: Include a mix of pleasure, mastery, and social activities.
  • Schedule self-care: Include basic self-care activities like showering, eating regular meals, and getting enough sleep.
  • Don't wait for motivation: Do the activity even if you don't feel like it. Often, motivation follows action.
  • Notice patterns: Pay attention to which activities improve your mood the most.
  • Be flexible: If you don't complete an activity, don't be hard on yourself. Just reschedule it.
  • Gradually increase difficulty: As you build momentum, slowly add more challenging activities.

Reflection

At the end of each week, take some time to reflect on your activities and their impact on your mood.

Weekly Reflection Questions Your Answers
Which activities did you enjoy the most?
Which activities gave you the greatest sense of accomplishment?
Did any activities surprise you (were more/less enjoyable than expected)?
What patterns do you notice about activities and your mood?
What activities would you like to do more of next week?
What obstacles made it difficult to complete activities?
How might you overcome these obstacles next week?
