Body Scan Meditation Script

The body scan meditation is a practice that involves systematically bringing attention to different parts of your body, from your feet to the top of your head. This practice helps develop body awareness, release tension, and cultivate mindfulness. It can be particularly helpful for reducing stress, managing pain, and improving sleep quality.

How to Use This Script

1. Find a quiet, comfortable space where you won't be disturbed for at least 20-30 minutes.

2. You can practice this meditation lying down on your back or sitting in a comfortable position.

3. Read through the script once before beginning to familiarize yourself with the flow.

4. You may record yourself reading the script to guide your practice, or have someone else read it to you.

5. Move through the body scan at a pace that feels right for you, allowing yourself to fully experience each section.

Note: Text in blue bold indicates suggested pauses. Take longer pauses between sections to allow yourself to fully experience the sensations in each area of your body.

Preparation

Find a comfortable position, either lying down on your back or sitting in a chair with your feet flat on the floor. If you're lying down, allow your arms to rest at your sides, palms facing up. If you're sitting, rest your hands on your thighs. [Pause for 5 seconds]

Gently close your eyes if that feels comfortable, or maintain a soft gaze downward. [Pause for 3 seconds]

Take a moment to notice the points of contact between your body and the surface beneath you. Feel the support it offers. [Pause for 10 seconds]

Now, bring awareness to your breath. There's no need to change your breathing in any way—simply notice the natural rhythm of your inhales and exhales. [Pause for 15 seconds]

As we begin this body scan practice, set an intention to approach your experience with curiosity and kindness. Whatever sensations, thoughts, or emotions arise, simply acknowledge them without judgment. [Pause for 10 seconds]

The Body Scan

Feet and Legs

Bring your awareness to your feet. Notice any sensations in your toes, the soles of your feet, the tops of your feet, and your ankles. [Pause for 10 seconds]

You might notice tingling, temperature, pressure, or perhaps no particular sensation at all. Whatever you experience is perfectly fine. [Pause for 10 seconds]

Now, move your attention to your lower legs—your shins, calves, and knees. Notice any sensations present here. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Continue moving upward to your thighs. Feel the weight of your legs against the surface beneath you. Notice any areas of tension or ease. [Pause for 15 seconds]

If you notice any discomfort or tension, see if you can breathe into that area, allowing it to soften with each exhale. [Pause for 10 seconds]

Pelvis and Abdomen

Bring your awareness to your pelvis, hips, and buttocks. Notice the points of contact with the surface beneath you. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Move your attention to your lower abdomen. Notice any sensations here—perhaps the gentle movement of your breath, or digestive activity. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Continue to your upper abdomen and solar plexus. Notice how this area expands with each inhale and contracts with each exhale. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Chest and Back

Bring your awareness to your chest. Feel the rise and fall of your breath here. Notice your heartbeat if you can. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Now, focus on your back—from your lower back, to your mid-back, to your upper back. Notice any areas of tension or ease. [Pause for 20 seconds]

With each exhale, imagine any tension in your back melting away, allowing your spine to lengthen and your back muscles to relax. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Arms and Hands

Bring your awareness to your shoulders. Notice if you're holding any tension here and allow your shoulders to soften and drop away from your ears. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Move your attention down your arms to your elbows, forearms, wrists, and finally to your hands and fingers. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Notice any sensations in your hands—perhaps tingling, warmth, or coolness. Allow your hands to be soft and receptive. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Neck and Head

Bring your awareness to your neck and throat. Notice any tension here and allow it to release with each exhale. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Move your attention to your jaw. Many of us hold tension in our jaw without realizing it. Allow your jaw to soften, with your teeth slightly apart and your tongue resting comfortably in your mouth. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Bring awareness to your face—your mouth, nose, cheeks, and eyes. Allow any tension in your face to melt away. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Now, focus on your forehead and scalp. Notice any sensations here and allow this area to soften and relax. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Whole Body Awareness

Now, expand your awareness to include your entire body at once. Feel the boundary of your skin, the weight of your body against the surface beneath you, and the gentle rhythm of your breath moving through you. [Pause for 20 seconds]

Notice how your body feels now compared to when you began this practice. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Take a few moments to appreciate your body and the awareness you've cultivated during this practice. [Pause for 15 seconds]

Closing the Practice

As we prepare to end this body scan meditation, take a deep breath in through your nose. [Pause for 3 seconds]

And exhale fully through your mouth. [Pause for 3 seconds]

Take another deep breath in. [Pause for 3 seconds]

And exhale completely. [Pause for 3 seconds]

Begin to gently wiggle your fingers and toes, reawakening movement in your body. [Pause for 5 seconds]

If you're lying down, you might roll onto one side before slowly coming up to a seated position. [Pause for 10 seconds]

When you're ready, gently open your eyes, taking a moment to transition back to your surroundings. [Pause for 5 seconds]

Take a moment to notice how you feel now, carrying this awareness with you as you continue with your day. [Pause for 10 seconds]

Tips for a Successful Practice

  • Consistency: Try to practice regularly, even if it's just for a few minutes each day.
  • Patience: If your mind wanders (which is completely normal), gently bring your attention back to the part of the body you were focusing on.
  • Non-judgment: Approach your experience with curiosity rather than criticism.
  • Comfort: Make adjustments to your position if needed to stay comfortable.
  • Timing: A full body scan typically takes 20-45 minutes, but you can adapt the practice to fit the time you have available.
  • Falling asleep: If you tend to fall asleep during the practice, try doing it sitting up or at a time when you're more alert.

Adaptations: This script can be modified for different needs. For a shorter practice, spend less time on each body part. For pain management, spend more time breathing into areas of discomfort. For sleep preparation, practice in bed and allow yourself to drift off if sleep comes.
